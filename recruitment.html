Open Positions for Postdoc Researchers in Formal Methods and Quantum Computing

Announced in November 2024. We are looking for passionate researchers to tackle foundational and applied challenges in formal methods and quantum computing.

The Institute of Information Science, Academia Sinica in Taipei, Taiwan, invites applications for postdoctoral researcher positions under the supervision of Prof. Yu-Fang Chen. This role offers a unique opportunity to engage in fundamental research at the intersection of formal methods and quantum computing within Taiwan’s leading research institution.

We Are Looking for Candidates for a Position at the Intersection of Formal Methods and Quantum Computing

The ideal candidate will have a strong background in formal methods, quantum computing, or related fields and will be passionate about bridging theoretical research with practical applications, particularly in the realm of quantum technologies.

  • Technically Proficient: Strong expertise in formal methods, quantum computing, or related areas.
  • Ambitious and Open-Minded: Eager to apply theoretical research to real-world challenges in quantum computing.
  • Collaborative and Independent: Able to work effectively both independently and as part of a dynamic, collaborative team.

The position offers significant opportunities for academic growth, collaboration, and exposure to cutting-edge real-world applications. The candidate will focus on developing formal techniques for analyzing and verifying quantum programs. Ｔhe duration of the job can be up to five years.

References:

  • An Automata-Based Framework for Verification and Bug Hunting in Quantum Circuits (PLDI 2023, QPL 2023, invited to submit as a CACM research highlights article) – Foundational framework.
  • AutoQ: An Automata-Based Quantum Circuit Verifier (CAV 2023) – Tool and symbolic extension.
  • Verifying Quantum Circuits with Level-Synchronized Tree Automata (POPL 2025) – Specialized tree automata model for quantum verification.
  • AutoQ 2.0: From Verification of Quantum Circuits to Verification of Quantum Programs (under submission) – Expanding verification from circuits to programs.
  • A Theory of Cartesian Arrays with Applications in Quantum Circuit Verification (CADE 2023) – Developing SMT theory for quantum.
  • Accelerating Quantum Circuit Simulation with Symbolic Execution and Loop Summarization (ICCAD 2024) – MTBDD-based circuit simulation with loop summarization.

Application Process

To apply, please send the following materials to yfc@iis.sinica.edu.tw with the subject line: Postdoc Application – Formal Methods and Quantum Computing:

  1. A brief CV.
  2. A short description of research interests (1–2 pages, informal descriptions are welcome).
  3. A list of publications (a dblp or Google Scholar link suffices).
  4. PDF copies of two representative papers.
  5. Contact information for two or more references.

Applications will be reviewed on a rolling basis. The position will remain open until filled. Early applications are encouraged.
